<p><p>The crystalline dextrose market is primarily segmented into two types: monohydrate glucose and anhydrous glucose. Monohydrate glucose, containing one water molecule per glucose unit, is commonly used in food and beverage applications for sweetness and energy. Anhydrous glucose, on the other hand, lacks water, making it a more concentrated form that is favored in pharmaceuticals and dietary supplements. Both types serve distinct industry needs, influencing their demand and pricing in the market.</p></p>
