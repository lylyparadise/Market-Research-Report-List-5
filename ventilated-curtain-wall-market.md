<p><p>A ventilated curtain wall is a building façade system that consists of a non-structural outer covering, which is designed to provide thermal insulation and protection from environmental elements while allowing for air circulation. This system typically features an air gap between the external cladding and the building's structural wall, enabling effective ventilation and moisture control. The growing demand for energy-efficient building solutions and sustainable architectural designs is driving the expansion of the ventilated curtain wall market.</p><p>As urbanization continues to rise globally, the need for innovative building materials that enhance energy performance has become increasingly important. <p><p>The ventilated curtain wall market encompasses two primary types: unitised and stick built systems. Unitised type involves pre-fabricated panels that are assembled in a factory and installed as single units, providing quick installation and high-quality control. In contrast, stick built type is constructed on-site with individual components, allowing for more design flexibility but typically requiring more labor and time. Both systems improve building aesthetics, energy efficiency, and protection against environmental factors, catering to diverse architectural needs.</p></p>
