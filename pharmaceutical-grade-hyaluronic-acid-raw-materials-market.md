<p><p>The pharmaceutical-grade hyaluronic acid raw materials market includes two primary types: microbial fermentation and animal tissue sources. Microbial fermentation involves the bioengineering of bacteria to produce hyaluronic acid, offering a consistent and sustainable supply with minimal risk of contamination. In contrast, animal tissue-derived hyaluronic acid is sourced from animal connective tissues, such as rooster combs, but may pose higher risks of impurities and ethical concerns. Both types cater to various applications in pharmaceuticals, aesthetics, and regenerative medicine.</p></p>
