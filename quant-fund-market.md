<p><p>Quant funds can be classified into several market types. Trend following funds capitalize on persistent market movements by buying assets in an upward trend and selling those in a downward trend. Countertrend strategies seek to profit from price reversals against prevailing trends. Statistical arbitrage funds exploit pricing inefficiencies between correlated securities. Convertible arbitrage involves trading convertible securities against their underlying stocks. Fixed income arbitrage targets price discrepancies in bonds, while commodity spread trades focus on relative pricing between commodities. Other strategies include various algorithmic trading methods.</p></p>
