<p><p>The apple cider vinegar market is primarily divided into two types: filtered and unfiltered. Filtered apple cider vinegar undergoes a clarification process, resulting in a clear liquid with a more refined taste, often preferred for culinary uses and cocktails. Unfiltered apple cider vinegar retains the "mother," a colony of beneficial bacteria, offering a cloudier appearance and a more robust flavor. Consumers seeking health benefits tend to favor unfiltered varieties, while filtered options are popular for general cooking and aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
