<p><p>The sophorolipid market is divided into two main types: lactonic and acidic sophorolipids. Lactonic sophorolipids are characterized by their lactone structure, offering greater stability and making them suitable for cosmetic and personal care applications. Acidic sophorolipids, on the other hand, possess a free carboxylic acid group, making them effective surfactants and antimicrobial agents. Both types are derived from renewable resources and are gaining traction in industries like pharmaceuticals, food, and agriculture due to their environmentally friendly properties.</p></p>
