<p><p>Amalgam fillings, commonly used in dental restorations, are categorized into two main types: low-copper amalgam and high-copper amalgam. Low-copper amalgam contains less copper and is prone to corrosion, which can compromise its longevity. In contrast, high-copper amalgam features a higher copper content, enhancing its strength, durability, and resistance to corrosion. This advancement results in improved clinical outcomes, making high-copper amalgam the preferred choice in modern dentistry for treating cavities and restoring dental function.</p></p>
