<p><p>The stainless steel alloy market is primarily categorized into three types: austenitic, ferritic, and martensitic. Austenitic alloys, known for their high corrosion resistance and versatility, dominate various applications, especially in food and chemical industries. Ferritic alloys offer good resistance to oxidation and stress corrosion at lower costs, often used in automotive and industrial applications. Martensitic alloys, known for their strength and hardness, find use in applications requiring high wear resistance, such as cutlery and industrial tools.</p></p>
