<p><p>Laminated glass consists of multiple layers bonded together for enhanced safety and durability. PVB (Polyvinyl Butyral) is the most common interlayer, offering excellent adhesion, clarity, and impact resistance. EVA (Ethylene Vinyl Acetate) provides superior UV resistance and is often used in decorative applications. SGP (SentryGlas Plus) is a high-performance interlayer with exceptional strength and rigidity, ideal for structural applications. Other interlayers include specialized materials like TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane) or resin-based options, catering to niche requirements such as soundproofing or extreme durability. Each type serves distinct market needs.</p></p>
