<p><p>The hydrophilic catheters market features two primary types of coatings: Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP)-based coatings and Hyaluronic Acid (HA)-based coatings. PVP-based coatings enhance lubricity and reduce friction during catheter insertion, improving patient comfort and minimizing trauma. In contrast, HA-based coatings leverage the natural properties of hyaluronic acid to provide enhanced biocompatibility and moisture retention, further promoting healing and reducing infection risks. Both types aim to improve catheter performance and patient outcomes while addressing specific clinical needs.</p></p>
