<p><p>Meat glue can be sourced from different types of markets - animal, plant, and microbial. Animal-derived meat glue is typically made from collagen extracted from animals like cows or pigs. Plant-based meat glue is derived from sources like seaweed or soybeans. Microbial meat glue is produced using enzymes derived from microorganisms. Each type of meat glue serves the same purpose of binding pieces of meat together to create a cohesive protein structure.</p></p>
