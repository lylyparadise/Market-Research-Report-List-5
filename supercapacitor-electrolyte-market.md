<p><p>The supercapacitor electrolyte market is primarily divided into two types: aqueous solutions and organic solvents. Aqueous solutions, typically consisting of water-based electrolytes, offer high ionic conductivity and safety, making them suitable for various applications. However, they have lower voltage limits. Conversely, organic solvents provide higher energy density and broader operating voltage ranges, enhancing supercapacitor performance, but may pose safety and environmental concerns. Each type serves distinct needs in energy storage applications, balancing efficiency, safety, and operational limits.</p></p>
