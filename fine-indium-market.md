<p><p>The fine indium market comprises two main types: native indium and regenerated indium. Native indium is extracted directly from mineral ores, often as a byproduct of zinc refining, and is characterized by its high purity level. Regenerated indium, on the other hand, is obtained through recycling indium from end-of-life products, such as electronics and solar panels. Both types serve diverse industrial applications, with native indium typically commanding higher prices due to its mining costs, while regenerated indium supports sustainability initiatives.</p></p>
