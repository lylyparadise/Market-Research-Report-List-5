<p><p>A Hall-effect speed sensor is a device that detects the speed of a rotating object, such as a wheel or a shaft, by utilizing the Hall effect principle. When a magnetic field interacts with an electric current flowing through a conductor, it generates a voltage perpendicular to both the magnetic field and the direction of the current. This induced voltage can be measured and correlated to the speed of the object. Hall-effect speed sensors are commonly used in automotive applications, industrial machinery, and various consumer devices for accurate speed measurement and feedback.</p><p>The Hall-effect Speed Sensor Market is anticipated to experience significant growth, with expectations to expand at a CAGR of 5.8% during the forecast period.
