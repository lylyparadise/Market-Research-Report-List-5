<p><p>Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy (CRT) systems primarily consist of two market types: CRT pacemakers and CRT defibrillators. CRT pacemakers deliver synchronized electrical impulses to the heart, improving its rhythm and efficiency, particularly in patients with heart failure. In contrast, CRT defibrillators combine resynchronization therapy with defibrillation capabilities, providing immediate shock therapy in cases of life-threatening arrhythmias. Both devices aim to enhance cardiac function, reduce symptoms, and improve patients' quality of life, addressing varying clinical needs in heart disease management.</p></p>
