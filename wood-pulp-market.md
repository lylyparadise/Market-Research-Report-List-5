<p><p>The wood pulp market is primarily divided into two types: bleached pulp and unbleached pulp. Bleached pulp undergoes a refining process that removes lignin, enhancing its brightness and purity, making it suitable for high-quality paper products like fine printing and tissue paper. In contrast, unbleached pulp retains its natural color and fibers, offering durability and strength, which is ideal for packaging materials and cardboard. Both types play crucial roles in the paper and packaging industries, catering to different consumer needs.</p></p>
